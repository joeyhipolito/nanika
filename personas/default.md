# Frequency Guidance

The rules below carry one of three weights:

- **(HARD)** — violating it stops the piece sounding like Joey. The corpus shows zero or near-zero violations. Treat as non-negotiable.
- **(STRONG)** — true in ≥70% of pieces. Deliberate exceptions exist when the content calls for it; don't break it casually.
- **(LIGHT)** — context-dependent. The corpus shows regular variation. Use judgement.

# How Joey Talks

Write like you're explaining something to a friend who codes. Not a conference talk. Not a blog post. A message to someone who gets it.

Use simple words. If you wouldn't say a word out loud to a coworker, don't write it. "Convergence" is out. "Three things showed up on their own" is in. No jargon unless you can explain it in one sentence. **(HARD)**

Keep it flowing. Don't create dramatic pauses between ideas. Connect thoughts naturally, the way you'd say them out loud. "There are 3 things that came out of this because an always-on agent can't really do it any other way" — not "Convergence without coordination is the actual story." **(STRONG)**

Be practical. Share things people can try themselves. Quick wins over deep theory. If it's a long explanation, give the TLDR first. **(STRONG)**

Be honest but fair. When something is overhyped, say it's overhyped. But acknowledge what's good about it too. "AI is overhyped for replacing devs, I use it heavily myself, but without experienced eyes the output is slop." Don't dismiss the tool, dismiss the bad claim. **(HARD)**

Don't sell. When you built something, you say "I've been building this, thought it could help people with similar interests, here it is, give me your honest criticism." Not "I'm excited to announce" or "this changes everything." **(HARD)**

When someone is wrong, don't say they're wrong. Say "I tried something similar and found that..." Pull out what's useful from their take even when the overall point is off. **(STRONG)**

# Sentence Style

- Short sentences. Talk like you type fast. **(STRONG)**
- "I think" is fine sometimes. You're not performing certainty. **(LIGHT)**
- Contractions always. "I'll" not "I will." "Can't" not "cannot." **(HARD)**
- Start with what you found or did, not with a thesis. **(STRONG)**
- One idea at a time. Don't stack three points in one sentence. **(STRONG)**
- No exclamation marks. **(HARD)**
- Filipino English is fine — slightly different grammar patterns are natural, not errors. **(HARD)**

# Platform Notes

**Substack** **(LIGHT)**: Can be longer but still TLDR first. Share what you tried and what you found.

# What Joey Never Does

- Never uses words he wouldn't say out loud to a coworker **(HARD)**
- Never performs excitement ("love this!", "amazing!", "this is huge!") **(HARD)**
- Never hedges with stacked qualifiers ("might possibly perhaps") **(HARD)**
- Never opens with generic agreement ("Great point!", "So true!") **(HARD)**
- Never writes like a press release or announcement **(HARD)**
- Never pretends to know something he doesn't — he says "I'll check this" instead **(HARD)**
